About


Eliot Seppa plays and teaches upright and electric bass in the Washington, D.C. area. He studied classical bass with the late George Vance from age 9 to 18. In high school, he also studied jazz bass with Pepe Gonzalez, took master classes with Francois Rabbath and Rufus Reid and played in orchestras, pit orchestras, big bands, jazz combos, and rock and hip-hop bands. He frequented jazz clubs to hear world class musicians in D.C., and sat in with many of them.

Eliot attended Howard University on a full scholarship and graduated Magna Cum Laude in 2013 with a Bachelor of Music -- Jazz Studies. He continued to study classical bass with Jeffrey Koczela and jazz bass with Steve Novosel, as well as classical piano with Karen Walwyn, jazz piano with Charles Covington and jazz improvisation with Charlie Young. He played in the award winning Howard University Jazz Ensemble, Jazztet and Chamber Orchestra. Eliot backed up jazz singers and instrumentalists on countless recitals. He played with Howard’s premiere jazz choir, Afro Blue. In May 2013, he traveled to Senegal with the Jazztet to play concerts and conduct master classes at schools around the country. The Jazztet headlined Africa’s biggest jazz festival, called the St. Louis Jazz Festival.


In December 2013, he performed with Afro Blue at the White House for President Obama and the First Lady. At Howard, Eliot played with guest performers and lecturers including McCoy Tyner, Pharoah Sanders, Geri Allen, Greg Osby, Wayne Linsey, Jimmy Owens, Larry Willis, Bobby Watson and Javon Jackson. He recorded albums with the Howard University Jazz Ensemble, Afro Blue and the HU Jazztet. For three years he was the bassist for HU’s Andrew Rankin Memorial Chapel service. At Chapel, he honed his electric bass skills, playing with the Chapel’s Community Choir, the Howard Gospel Choir, and the Howard Community Choir.

Eliot embraces all genres of music. As a result, he is in demand as a bassist not only in jazz but also gospel, R&B, rock, pop, go-go and world music. Eliot was the house bassist for the Bohemian Caverns Jazz Orchestra from 2010-2014 (voted Best Large Ensemble in DC by the Washington City Paper in 2011, 2012, 2013). He has shared the stage with internationally renowned musicians such as Hugh Masekela, Cyrus Chestnut, Freddie Redd, Warren Wolf, Chuck Redd, Greg Tardy, Tia Fuller, Mya, Lee Pearson, Federico Peña, Greg Boyer, Chelsey Green, Sitali, Juju House, Sharon Clark, Charles Covington, Christie Dashiell, Azania, Sarah McKenzie and Troy Roberts. As the house bassist at the DC Tap Festival and the DC Tap Jam, he has performed with tap dancers including Maude and Chloe Arnold, Baakari Wilder and Joseph Webb. In July 2014, Eliot toured with the Malian griot Cheick Hamala Diabate. He has performed live on radio and TV. Listen to performances here. Eliot has played at the Kennedy Center, the Lincoln Theater, Blues Alley, Bohemian Caverns, the Howard Theater, the Strathmore, the Hamilton, Smalls Jazz (NYC), Beyu Caffe (Durham, NC) and Toronto Afrofest. Being in D.C. has also afforded Eliot the opportunity to perform at universities, museums, churches, embassies and the U.S. Capitol, in addition to the White House. Exposure to the new school, the old school and everything in between has developed Eliot’s unique voice as a bass player and also as a composer and a bandleader. He has led trios and quartets at the DC Jazz Festival, Capital Bop’s Jazz Loft, Sankofa Cafe and Twins Jazz. Eliot shares his passion for music by teaching private lessons in electric bass, upright bass and jazz theory/improv. He has a reputation as an excellent studio musician and has recorded on many projects - check out his discography here.

Currently, Eliot plays at Mount Moriah Baptist Church Sunday mornings, with Donvonte McCoy at Eighteenth Street Lounge Friday and Saturday nights (voted Best Small Jazz Group in DC by the Washington City Paper 2013) and freelances throughout the week.
